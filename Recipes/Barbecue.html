Barbecue Recipe

Description

Barbecue, or BBQ, is a method of cooking meat slowly over low heat, often using smoke from wood or charcoal to add flavor. This technique makes the meat tender and gives it a smoky taste. Different regions have their own styles: in the U.S., Texas, Memphis, and Carolina are famous for unique meats and sauces, while Argentina has Asado, Korea has Gogigui, and Japan has Yakiniku. Commonly used meats include beef, pork, chicken, and sometimes lamb. BBQ often involves rubs, marinades, or sauces, and is as much a social gathering as it is a cooking method, bringing people together outdoors to enjoy food and company.

Steps

  1. Choose your meat
  2. Prepare the meat
  3. Preheat the grill or smoker
  4. Set up for indirect heat
  5. Place the meat on the grill
  6. Cook slowly
  7. Monitor temperature
  8. Add smoke or baste
  9. Rest and meat
  10. Serve and enjoy
